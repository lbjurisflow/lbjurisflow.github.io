What Is the AI Intake System?

The LB JurisFlow AI Intake System is the first module of the Sovereign Law Firm Engine. It replaces the single most expensive failure point in every PI firm: the missed lead. When a potential client calls your firm at 11 PM after a truck accident, your AI Intake System answers — immediately, intelligently, and without waking a single member of your staff.

The Intake Leak costs the average PI firm $500,000–$2.1M per missed catastrophic case. The AI Intake System closes that gap permanently.

How It Works

Every inbound contact — call, SMS, web form, referral portal — enters the intake engine automatically. The local AI conducts a structured interview: injury type, jurisdiction, liability facts, urgency indicators. Each lead is scored and classified in real time.

Why ChatGPT Is a Time Bomb for PI Law Firms — And What to Do About It

Published by Umar Iqbal · May 2025 · 8 min read

The Quiet Crisis in Your Firm Right Now

Right now — today — someone at your Personal Injury law firm is pasting protected health information into a public AI tool. They are not doing it maliciously. They are doing it because they are overwhelmed, they have a motion due in four hours, and ChatGPT is faster than reading 300 pages of medical records manually.

They mean well. They are your best paralegal. And they just created a potential bar complaint, a HIPAA violation, and an argument for opposing counsel that your client's attorney-client privilege has been waived — all in 45 seconds.

This is the ChatGPT Privilege Bomb. It is detonating silently inside PI firms across the United States, and the vast majority of managing partners have no idea it is happening.

What the Law Says

The attorney-client privilege protects confidential communications between attorneys and their clients. Both protections can be waived — intentionally or inadvertently — when protected information is disclosed to a third party without appropriate controls. When your paralegal pastes a client's medical records into ChatGPT, that information is transmitted to OpenAI's servers. OpenAI is a third party.

The Only Complete Solution

The sovereign solution is simple in concept: move your AI infrastructure inside your building. When your AI runs on hardware you own, in a building you control, on a network that does not connect to external servers, there is no third-party disclosure. The privilege is preserved by physical architecture.

This is what LB JurisFlow installs. Not a policy document telling your staff not to use ChatGPT. Physical infrastructure that makes the question irrelevant — because the local AI your staff uses is better, faster, and available for everything your firm needs.

n8n + Ollama: The Sovereign Tech Stack Every PI Firm Needs to Understand

Published by Umar Iqbal · February 2025 · 10 min read

Two Tools. One Sovereign Stack.

To understand how LB JurisFlow works technically, you need to understand two tools: n8n and Ollama. Together, they form the engine that powers sovereign AI inside your law firm.

What is n8n?

n8n is an open-source workflow automation platform — similar to Zapier, but self-hosted. When we deploy n8n inside your firm, it runs on your local server. It is the orchestration layer — the system that connects your AI models, your CRM, your calendar, your document storage, and your case management system.

What is Ollama?

Ollama is an open-source platform for running large language models locally. It allows you to run GPT-class AI models on your own hardware without any internet connectivity required. When your LB JurisFlow system processes a 2,000-page medical record, Ollama is the component doing the reading and analysis. Nothing touches the internet.

How They Work Together

n8n handles the workflow logic: when to run what, what data to pass where, what to do with the output. Ollama handles the AI inference: reading documents, understanding language, generating structured analysis. Combined with your local hardware, they form a complete sovereign AI infrastructure.

Legal Ethics

Attorney-Client Privilege in the Age of AI: What Every PI Lawyer Must Know

Published by Umar Iqbal · January 2025 · 7 min read

The Privilege Problem Is Real

Attorney-client privilege is the foundation of the legal profession. AI is putting it at risk in ways most attorneys do not yet fully understand.

The core issue: Attorney-client privilege protects communications that remain confidential. When protected communications are processed by a third-party system — including cloud-based AI tools — the confidentiality question becomes complicated.

The Voluntary Disclosure Problem

Courts have consistently held that privilege can be waived by voluntary disclosure to third parties. Data transmitted to external servers, processed by third-party AI systems, potentially stored in training datasets, and accessible to vendor employees under certain circumstances — all of these create risk.

The Sovereign Solution

The cleanest solution is architectural: run your AI on infrastructure you own and control. When your AI processes a deposition summary, a medical record, or a client communication on your local server — hardware physically located inside your office — there is no third-party disclosure. The privilege analysis is straightforward.
